PLASTIC INFO

  • * plastics can be recycled. The simplest way to remember which plastics can currently be recycled is to
    remember the following phrase: "If the item has a #1 or #2
    on the bottom and the neck is smaller than the base, you can recycle it!"
  • * Please don't put any of the following plastic items in your recycling bin:
    motor oil containers, yogurt and margarine tubs even if it has a #1 or #2, or microwave trays. Reuse plastics
    instead of throwing them away and purchasing new. Some shops allow to return worn out bags for new ones.
  • * Plastics last for hundred of years. Worst, they tend to be broken down into micro plastics which can have devastating effects on marine life
    and animals on top of those food chains.
  • * In addition,it requires lots of fossil fuels to make and releases
    tonns of pollutants in the air. This is compounded by the fact that
    different plastics do not mix well and cannot be recycled.
  • * Plastics are also very easily tossed away rather than recycled/reused and end up in the ocean.
